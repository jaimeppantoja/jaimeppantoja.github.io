Vancouver Housing Report

November 2024

This report delivers a detailed analysis of the Vancouver real estate market, emphasizing current pricing trends and property distributions. Utilizing data sourced from Zillow, the analysis is based on 800 observations from a broader dataset of over 4,000 listed properties in Vancouver. As of November 18, 2024, this dataset provides a solid basis for examining market dynamics and supporting strategic decision-making.

The report goes beyond offering a snapshot of the current market, aiming to provide actionable insights that empower investors, real estate professionals, and potential buyers to make well-informed decisions. By integrating diverse analytical methods, it delivers a thorough and detailed exploration of the Vancouver real estate landscape, uncovering trends and patterns that inform strategic planning and decision-making.

Pricing Analysis

This section offers a complete analysis of the Vancouver real estate market, focusing on the overall price distribution and its segmentation across various property types. The general price distribution highlights prevalent price ranges, outliers, and market trends, providing valuable insights for a wide range of stakeholders.

Distribution of Housing Prices

The histogram provides a clear visual representation of housing price distribution in Vancouver, revealing a heavily left-skewed pattern. This highlights the prevalence of lower-priced homes alongside a smaller proportion of highly expensive properties—a characteristic often observed in housing markets, especially in cities with elevated housing costs.

The histogram reveals a left-skewed distribution, indicating that the majority of properties fall within a relatively "affordable" price range, while a smaller number of luxury properties are situated on the higher end of the scale. In the context of such distributions, it is essential to note that the mean can be a less reliable measure of central tendency. This is due to the influence of outliers, such as high-priced luxury properties, which can significantly inflate the mean and shift it away from the bulk of the data. Therefore, the median emerges as a more robust indicator for representing the typical house price in the city, as it remains unaffected by extreme values and provides a more accurate reflection of the central tendency within the dataset.

Housing Prices by Property Type

Following the overall market trend, the distribution of property prices is distinctly left-skewed, reflecting a concentration of lower-priced properties with a smaller proportion extending into the luxury segment. However, pricing dynamics vary significantly across property types, giving a complete view of the Vancouver real estate market. Houses stand out as the most expensive category, characterized by the highest median and mean prices, alongside a pronounced right skew that highlights a substantial share of high-priced listings. In contrast, condos feature lower median and mean prices and a less skewed distribution, indicating a more balanced and accessible price range.

Multi-family homes occupy the middle ground in terms of pricing, with moderate skewness suggesting a mix of both affordable and high-value properties. Townhouses, meanwhile, show relatively lower median and mean prices compared to houses, with a more evenly distributed price range that appeals to a broader demographic. These observations highlight the segmented nature of the market: houses predominantly serve high-end buyers, while condos and townhouses provide more affordable options. The presence of high-priced properties across all categories underscores the potential for luxury investment opportunities, making Vancouver's real estate market attractive to a diverse spectrum of buyers and investors.

Distribution of Property Types in Vancouver

This section delivers a thorough analysis of Vancouver's housing market, showcasing its diverse offerings tailored to varying buyer preferences and lifestyles.
